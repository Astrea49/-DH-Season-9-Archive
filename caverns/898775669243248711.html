Near the top of the caverns, an outshoot of the #cavern-entrance opens up to this area filled with trash. It's fairly open, and the trash pile fills up to the top of the cave and towers above everyone. Water also floods the bottom of this cave, so the only way in is parkouring across the islands of trash. Piles of broken electronics, cars, furnishings, and anything else that you could throw away can be found here. The list ranges from old bubblegum wrappers to moldy bananas. The cavern opening itself is fairly large, letting light in from an opening above. Combined with the flooding of water at the bottom of the cavern, this allows mold and small plants to grow here. Notably, there is a lack of fauna. The water itself can also get fairly deep at 7 feet deep. Towards the bottom of the trash heap is mainly larger items such as cars that you can climb up on. None of the electronics here work, but there are some useful items here. You won't be able to build something like a radio anyways. Not that it'll work. The smell of oil, rusting metals, and just straight up trash pollutes this place. You can tell that this area could've been something amazing if only it hadn't been filled with trash. Items from throughout the centuries are here, not just modern day things. Wooden baths from the 14th century, old clothes from the 16th. The most notable thing is a guillotine from 1789. (edited)
