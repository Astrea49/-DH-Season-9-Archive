The House of War. A place in which, just from the name itself, could only elude to a dorm filled with chaos and mayhem. War: conflict involving hostility between two opposing sides. It was quite humorous how the geologist had found herself residing within this dorm. A place in which conflict is what became the roots for the very concept of this building, and the seemingly calm, yet conflicted, girl had found herself as a resident within it. Upon walking in the first time hearing of the dorms, she was unable to truly have a reaction from the dorm. It was quite different from her normal room, her normal life. But this place was far from normal. Much like war, she would have to learn to strategize and adapt to the unfamiliar area of the jungle, and the unknown world of the killing game. If Monokuma was speaking the truth, then, much like war, blood will be spilled, trust will be severed, and her ordinary life of routine and organization will never return to her. She never really liked the interior of the house, however. The rougher, messier, more brutal aesthetic was something which never sat right with her. She was always someone who preferred the organization of her own room, which with time developed into her own mini research area. It was almost as if two sides of the coin were in play here, when comparing the geologist towards the look around the room. Organization and Disorder. However, perhaps with time, she will learn to adapt to the surroundings of this house.
Although one thing even she knew for certain she would never truly adjust to was the fireplace. It was such a small thing to focus on, yet it seemed fire had followed her with every step she took. The torch the group held whenever they were trapped within the cavern. The bonfire where she had first spoken to Wynona. And now, the fireplace within the center of the dorm. Fire had always followed her since the wreck of the boat. An unpredictable force which haunted her. Much like the shape and direction of the flames, it can mean a variety of things. Destruction like a wildfire, yet revival like a Phoenix. A force that can succumb you into despair, or reach into the depths of your soul for hope.
Erina had now found herself in one of the few watchtowers of the house. It overlooked the grand unknown of the darkest depths of the jungle. Occasionally, she would believe to see things scurrying around, only to tell herself she is imagining things. She would never admit it, but the fear of the unknown scenario truly scared her. It was something which out here truly terrified her. Fear is always a difficult concept to calm, especially when the winds of terror begin to howl within one’s mind. However, Erina had found a way to at least ease the fear, even just a little. She continued looking out into the dark jungle. And, subconsciously began to tap the railing of the watchtower. (I’m assuming there’s like a little railing to make sure no one falls out of said tower) She tapped the railing again and again, almost in a rhythmic pattern. Tap-Tap. Tap-Tap. The simple organized rhythm within the chaos of the watchtower. The monotonous melody of the tapping within the cacophony of the grand mystery of the jungle. While tapping on the railing, she let her mind begin to wander a bit. The simple, yet complicated, questions began to bubble and rise into her head once more. The questions in which she was unable to receive answers in this snapshot of time. However, Erina was patient. She could wait for the answers. All she had out here in the jungle was time, anyways.
