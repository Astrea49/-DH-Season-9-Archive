•······················• •······················• At last, the intruders were gone. October hated every second of talking to them. It was fun at first, but the fact that they just- barged in without a thought and threw her food on the ground, it hurt. This was normal. This is how everyone thought of her. Some annoying little prick who had the mental maturity of a six year old. At least she was used to it I guess..?? Old memories playing over and over in her head again, different from the ones she was used to here. Watchful glares. Scoffs. Rude words from her peers and teachers alike. The occasional rude shove or snicker. This is how it always was. But oh well. At least October could live with it. She would just.. never let them in again I guess. That made sense. They were rude, why give them what they want?? Why was she even dwelling on all those people? They were idiots, and undeserving of her time. He grumbled to himself as he gathered a few choice items around the house, being a candle and a few books and pieces of paper. Not to mention her trusty can of baked beans. And after finding a secluded spot in the house, October was ready. He started with a piece of the floor near the wall. Using all those books he found to set up an altar looking thing. Once all stacked up and clean, boom. Candles down. WAIT FUCK he forgot the tape. After said tape was acquired, October started flipping through his notebook. Looking for one specific page. One page she doodled on- RIP. He found it, obviously. Tearing it up and tacking it on the wall. The setup was crude, but the amount of heart it had was endearing.
On the wall sat the drawing, which had a stack of books lining the bottom. They were set up in such a way that the drawing was framed, and definitely the centerpiece. And last but not least, one candle. Sitting in the middle of the book stack. It wasn't lit yet- but that would change soon.
- But first, the beans. You really thought she was going to eat those?? Pff. You think she eats straight baked beans?? Only psycho murderers do that. Instead, she set up a second altar. In the corner of the room, away from the first altar. It wasn't as neat as the last, but there was something about it that said "do not touch". The beans can was on the top of the book stack, with a note tucked underneath.
God. She was running around a lot today, was she? But it was worth it. With a quick quest to the kitchen, she got the candle lit. And it was back on the altar. October was now sitting on the floor, curled up into a ball. Basking in the silence of the room as the candle burned down. But as boredom started creeping across her brain and anxieties bubbled up in her chest, she started to hum. Hum a tune that would always calm her down. The composer had a light voice, becoming increasingly more strained as he hummed. Tears bubbled up in his eyes- dribbling down his face. It was soon becoming more and more unbearable- harder and harder to sing by the moment. The faucets in her eyes that were just a slow trickle were now waterfalls, dampening his sleeves whenever he tried to wipe them up. Man- he cried way too easily, didn't he?? Hours passed and the candle burned down, and October continued humming. Humming until her tears ran dry and her eyelids started to flutter. Even now she couldn't fight the clutches of sleep, no matter how much she wanted to. Pathetic. Can't even stay awake for them, huh. He scooted over towards the candle, still sitting on the floor. Watery blue eyes stuck on the picture on the wall for a few moments. "I'm sorry." October's voice was hoarse. "You didn't deserve that. I'm sorry I was mean. I'm sorry I forgot your name. I hope you have a good next life, sir. Ultimate Explorer." October's fingers interlocked on impulse, wrapping tightly around one another until her knuckles were white and palms were pressed together. Then, after a few shaky moments of this prayer-like hand gesture, she blew out the candle. Shrouding the room in darkness once more. With a flump, the composer laid down on his back. He couldn't see anything- but he knew where the other altar was. And looking right at it, October spoke again. "Goodnight Theron. I miss you." Her eyelids fluttered closed, and she drifted off into another tumultuous sleep. •······················• •······················•
