Stepping into the pathways of the huge palace (or swimming in), the first thing you notice about this area is how relatively clean it is. The water here isn't polluted and mostly clear, though that might be the effects of the gateway. A huge chamber, fit for a king, sits inside of here. Kelp and coral grow freely, but they don't infest the place. In fact, they add to it. Areas along the sideline act like super fancy bleachers and can seat a thousand. (Not in the pic): Another thing notable about this place is the banquet table sitting in the center of the room. It stretches for most of the hall, easily being about 10 meters long. (Think the tables in the great hall in Harry Potter.) Old plates made of stone and coral mixed in with random utensils are scattered around the table. Above it, there are lights that would normally be lit with flame, but you're underwater. Instead, little orbs of light are used instead. You've never seen anything you. If you were to touch these orbs, you would feel a bit of warmth, but that's about it. However, it's obvious the centerpiece in here is the massive throne sitting at the end of the hall. It's absolutely massive, being made of marble and pearls. Unlike shown in the image, the throne is completely empty and you can sit in it fi you wanted it. A canopy covers overhead the throne, with curtains able to be used to veil the area from sight.
